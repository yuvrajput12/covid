How Virus Spread

How Contagion Coronavirus

The coronavirus, specifically known as SARS-CoV-2, is a highly contagious virus that causes the disease COVID-19. It primarily spreads from person to person through respiratory droplets when an infected individual coughs, sneezes, talks, or breathes. It can also spread by touching a surface or object that has the virus on it and then touching one's face, although this is less common.

  • Human Contact

    Close contact with an infected person, such as being within about 6 feet (2 meters) for a prolonged period, increases the risk of transmission.

  • Air Transmission

    Airborne transmission refers to the spread of infectious respiratory droplets or particles that remain suspended in the air and can travel distances beyond six feet

  • Contaminated Objects

    When an infected person coughs, sneezes, or touches their face, they can leave the virus on surfaces or objects they come into contact with. If another person touches these contaminated surfaces and then touches their face, specifically the mouth, nose, or eyes, they can potentially introduce the virus into their own body and become infected.

Corona Virus Symptoms

The symptoms of COVID-19, the illness caused by the coronavirus, can vary from mild to severe and may appear 2-14 days after exposure to the virus.

  • Coughing And Sneezing

    Coughing and sneezing are common symptoms of respiratory illnesses, including COVID-19.

  • Hot Fever

    Hot fever is a symptom often associated with various illnesses, including COVID-19.

  • Strong Headacke

    A strong headache can be a symptom of various conditions, including COVID-19.

  • Shortness Of Breath

    Shortness of breath is a potential symptom of respiratory illnesses, including COVID-19.

  • Confusion

    Confusion can be a symptom of various medical conditions, including infections, neurological disorders, and certain medications.

  • Sore Throat

    A sore throat is a common symptom of viral infections, such as the common cold or flu.

Prevention Coronavirus

Prevention of coronavirus includes practicing good hygiene, such as frequent handwashing, wearing masks, maintaining social distance, and following local health guidelines and regulations.

  • Wash Your Hands For 20sec

    Washing your hands for 20 seconds with soap and water is an effective way to remove germs and reduce the spread of infections.

  • Cover Nose and Mouth When Sneezing

    Covering your nose and mouth when sneezing helps prevent the spread of respiratory droplets and reduces the risk of transmitting infections to others.

  • Wear A Mask If Available

    Wearing a mask, if available, helps protect yourself and others by reducing the transmission of respiratory droplets and airborne particles.
